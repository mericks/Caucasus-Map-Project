The Caucasus

East meets West. Old meets New. Mountain meets Plain.

At the intersection of culture, language, and politics,
the Caucasus region, named for the eponymous mountain range that divides it,
represents the nexus of six major world cultures.

Historical Richness

A Profoundly Diverse Region

Many an empire has lay claim to the Caucasusian region, from Greeks and Mongols to Byzantines, Ottomans, and Soviets. Between the successive waves of conquerors and the natural isolation of mountain communities, the Caucasus is extraordinarily diverse both linguistically and ethnically.

Linguistic Diversity

More than Sixty Living Languages

Spoken among nearly as many peoples in an area scarcely larger than New England, these languages belong to six completely distinct linguistic families, with each family bearing almost no grammatical resemblance to the others.

Invisible Borders

Bounded by Religion, not Topography

The Caucasus traditionally has been divided into two parts - the North Caucasus and the South Caucasus - each lying on its respective side of the mountain range. However it is far more appropriate to imagine a boundary along an east-west axis, where religion has been the defining factor.
